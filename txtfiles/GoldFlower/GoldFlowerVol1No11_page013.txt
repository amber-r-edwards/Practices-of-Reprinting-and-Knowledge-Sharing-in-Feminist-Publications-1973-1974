IPEACE GROUPS CAMPAIGN  
TO REPEAL DRAFT LAWS  

Three organizations in the nine states of Mid-America are coordinating their efforts to repeal the draft in 1973. If no action is taken by Congress to extend it, the President's power to induct men into the armed services will end June 30, 1973. THIS DOES NOT END THE DRAFT. Peacetime conscription will continue in the form of required registration by all 18-year-old men; prosecution of those who fail to register, classification of all young men according to induction priority assigned to them, and the prosecution of those who refuse to deal with the bureaucracy.

Headlines University Conference  

This month Feminists of both sexes will have an unprecedented opportunity to get together with those of the same persuasion. University of Minnesota Women's Liberation will, on Feb. 2 and 3, sponsor a conference on The New Feminism: Cultural, Educational, Church Women and Political Implications. Feminist actions on a local, national, and international scale will be discussed. Guest speakers of national renown have been obtained. Present will be Flo Kennedy, black activist, civil rights lawyer, co-founder of NOW and the Feminist Party, and author of Abortion Rap, and Barbara Roberts, M.D., coordinator of the Women's National Abortion Action Coalition and part-time abortionist in her home state of Maryland. Also present will be an abortion rights activist from Canada, and a local black woman who will discuss forced sterilization.  

There will be a women's art display, and women poets and singers will present their work. There will be a multi-media show and a well-known feminist film will be aired.  

The function will be held in Coffman Memorial Union at the University of Minnesota. Admission will be inexpensive. For more information, give us a ring at 373-0033.  

The opportunity to not only pull the Feminist community together, but to be entertained and inspired is without parallel. All are welcome, all are urged to come and support it.
